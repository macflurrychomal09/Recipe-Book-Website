Paapdi Chaat

Papdi chaat is a popular Indian street food snack made with crispy papdis (fried dough wafers), tangy tamarind chutney, yogurt, and a variety of savory and spicy toppings such as chopped onions, tomatoes, potatoes, and chaat masala. It's typically garnished with cilantro leaves and sev (crunchy chickpea flour noodles). Papdi chaat offers a burst of flavors and textures, combining crunchy, tangy, and spicy elements, making it a favorite among chaat lovers.

Prepration time

  • Total:Approcimately 30 minutes
  • Prepration: 15 minutes
  • Cooking: 15 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Water for kneading
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 1/2 cup tamarind pulp
  • 1/4 cup jaggery or sugar
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Papdis (fried dough wafers)
  • Boiled potatoes, diced
  • Boiled chickpeas (optional)
  • Chopped onions
  • Chopped tomatoes
  • Plain yogurt
  • Green chutney (coriander-mint chutney)
  • Tamarind chutney (prepared earlier)
  • Chaat masala
  • Roasted cumin powder
  • Red chili powder (optional)
  • Sev (crunchy chickpea flour noodles)
  • Chopped fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instruction

1.

In a mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of semolina, 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt.

2.

Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a stiff dough. It should be firm and not too soft.

3.

Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a thin disc.

4.

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, fry the rolled discs until they turn golden brown and crisp. Remove them from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.

5.

In a saucepan, combine 1/2 cup of tamarind pulp, 1/4 cup of jaggery or sugar, 1 teaspoon of roasted cumin powder, 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste.

6.

Add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency. Simmer the mixture over medium heat for 5-7 minutes until it thickens slightly. Let it cool.

7.

Arrange the fried papdis on a serving plate.

8.

Top each papdi with diced boiled potatoes, boiled chickpeas (optional), chopped onions, and chopped tomatoes.

9.

Drizzle plain yogurt, green chutney (coriander-mint chutney), and tamarind chutney over the toppings.

10.

Sprinkle chaat masala, roasted cumin powder, and red chili powder (optional) on top for extra flavor.

10.

Garnish with a generous amount of sev (crunchy chickpea flour noodles) and chopped fresh cilantro leaves.
