Kurkura Momos

Kurkura momos are a crispy and flavorful variation of traditional momos, a popular Tibetan dumpling dish. These momos are typically filled with a savory mixture of vegetables or meat, wrapped in a thin dough, and then deep-fried until golden and crispy. The frying process gives them a crunchy texture, contrasting with the soft and juicy filling inside. Kurkura momos are often served with a spicy dipping sauce or chutney, making them a delicious and satisfying snack or appetizer.

Prepration time

  • Total:Approcimately 1 Hours
  • Prepration: 30 minutes
  • Cooking: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup finely chopped vegetables (such as cabbage, carrots, bell peppers)
  • 200 grams ground chicken or minced meat (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped onions
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar or lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil for sautéing
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup cornflour
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Instruction

1.

Prepare the filling, Heat a little oil in a pan and sauté onions until translucent. Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chili (if using), and sauté for another minute.

2.

Add chopped vegetables and cook until they are slightly tender. If using ground chicken or minced meat, add it now and cook until it's fully cooked.

3.

Stir in soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar or lemon juice, and salt. Cook for a few more minutes until the flavors are well combined. Remove from heat and let the filling cool down.

4.

Prepare the dough: In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

5.

Divide the dough into small equal-sized portions and roll each portion into a thin circle.

6.

Place a spoonful of the prepared filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the edges together and pinch them to seal, creating a pleated pattern around the edges.

7.

Prepare a batter by mixing all-purpose flour, cornflour, and water to a thick consistency. Dip each momo in the batter to coat evenly.

8.

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated momos in batches and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy.

9.

Remove the fried momos using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.

10.

Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce or chutney.
