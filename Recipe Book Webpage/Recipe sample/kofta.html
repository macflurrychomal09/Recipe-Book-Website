Malai Kofta

Malai Kofta is a popular Indian dish consisting of soft, creamy dumplings made from paneer (Indian cottage cheese) and potatoes, served in a rich and flavorful gravy made with tomatoes, onions, cream, and various spices. The dumplings are typically stuffed with nuts or raisins for added texture and flavor. Malai Kofta is known for its indulgent taste and luxurious texture, making it a favorite among vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike in Indian cuisine.

Prepration time

  • Total:Approcimately 2 Hours
  • Prepration: 45 minutes
  • Cooking: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, pistachios)
  • 2 tablespoons raisins
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup cashew nuts, soaked in water for 30 minutes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Instruction

1.

In a mixing bowl, combine grated paneer, mashed potatoes, all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger paste, garlic paste, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth dough.

2.

Divide the dough into equal-sized portions. If using filling, flatten each portion, place a small amount of filling in the center, and seal the edges to form a smooth ball. Repeat with the remaining dough.

3.

Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the kofta balls in batches and fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove them onto a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

4.

In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.

5.

Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.

6.

Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.

7.

Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.

8.

Drain and add soaked cashew nuts to the pan. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

9.

Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then transfer it to a blender and blend into a smooth paste.

10.

Return the blended mixture to the pan. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

11.

Pour in heavy cream and garam masala powder. Stir well and simmer for another 2-3 minutes.

12.

Add the fried kofta balls to the gravy and simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing them to absorb the flavors.

13.

Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or naan.
