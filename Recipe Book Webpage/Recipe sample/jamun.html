Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a popular Indian dessert made from khoya (reduced milk solids) or milk powder dough, fried into golden brown balls and soaked in a sweet syrup flavored with cardamom, saffron, and rose water. These soft, spongy balls are rich, indulgent, and often served warm, making them a favorite treat for celebrations and special occasions in Indian cuisine.

Prepration time

  • Total:Approcimately 1 Hours
  • Prepration: 45 minutes
  • Cooking: 45 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter), melted
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk or water (as needed)
  • A pinch of cardamom powder (optional)
  • Chopped nuts (such as almonds or pistachios) for garnishing (optional)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon rose water or kewra water (optional)

Instruction

1.

In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and cardamom powder (if using).

2.

Gradually add melted ghee to the dry ingredients and mix well. The mixture should resemble coarse crumbs.

3.

Slowly add milk or water, a little at a time, and knead the mixture into a smooth dough. Be careful not to over-knead.

4.

Divide the dough into small equal-sized portions and roll them into smooth balls. Make sure there are no cracks on the surface.

5.

Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan over medium heat. The oil should be hot but not smoking.

6.

Gently slide the rolled dough balls into the hot oil, frying them in batches. Stir occasionally and fry until they turn golden brown on all sides. Keep the heat medium-low to ensure even cooking and prevent burning.

7.

Once fried, remove the jamun from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil by placing them on a paper towel-lined plate.

8.

In a separate saucepan, combine sugar and water to make the sugar syrup. Add crushed cardamom pods and saffron strands (if using).

9.

Bring the syrup to a gentle boil, stirring occasionally until the sugar completely dissolves. Simmer for 5-7 minutes to thicken slightly.

10.

Return the blended mixture to the pan. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

11.

Allow the syrup to cool slightly, then gently add the fried jamun balls to the warm syrup.

12.

Let the jamun soak in the syrup for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the flavors and become soft and spongy.

13.

Garnish with chopped nuts (if desired) before serving.

13.

Serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature, and enjoy this delicious Indian dessert!
