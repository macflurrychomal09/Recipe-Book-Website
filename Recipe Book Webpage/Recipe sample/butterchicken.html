Butter Chicken

Butter chicken, also known as murgh makhani, is a popular Indian dish renowned for its rich and creamy flavor. Tender pieces of chicken are marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices, then cooked in a sauce made with butter, tomatoes, cream, and various aromatic spices such as garam masala, ginger, and garlic. The result is a luscious and indulgent dish with a perfect balance of savory and slightly sweet flavors. Butter chicken is often served with rice or naan bread, making it a beloved favorite among both Indian cuisine enthusiasts and newcomers alike.

Prepration time

  • Total:Approcimately 1 Hour
  • Prepration: 30 minutes
  • Cooking: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instruction

1.

Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, ginger paste, garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Add chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.

2.

Cook the chicken: In a large skillet or pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

3.

Prepare the sauce: In the same skillet, add the remaining butter and vegetable oil. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Stir in tomato puree and tomato paste, cooking until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate.

4.

Add spices: Add garam masala and kasuri methi to the sauce, stirring well to combine.

5.

Combine chicken and sauce: Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and mix it with the sauce, ensuring each piece is well coated.

6.

Simmer: Pour in the heavy cream and simmer for a few minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and chicken is fully cooked.

7.

Garnish and serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving. Serve hot with rice or naan bread.
