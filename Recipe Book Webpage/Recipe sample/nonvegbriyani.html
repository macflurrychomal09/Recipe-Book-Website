Non Veg Briyani

Non-veg biryani is a flavorful and aromatic dish that combines long-grain rice with tender pieces of meat, usually chicken, mutton, or seafood. It's a popular dish in South Asian cuisine, known for its rich blend of spices and layers of flavors. The meat is marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices before being cooked with fragrant basmati rice, creating a hearty and satisfying meal. Garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and sometimes boiled eggs, non-veg biryani is a beloved dish enjoyed by many for its complexity and depth of taste.

Prepration time

  • Total:Approcimately 2 Hours
  • Prepration: 1 Hour
  • Cooking: 1 Hour

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of meat (chicken, mutton, or seafood)
  • 2 cups of long-grain basmati rice
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup of plain yogurt
  • 4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust according to taste)
  • Handful of fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves, chopped
  • Handful of fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 4 tablespoons of cooking oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay leaves)
  • Ground spices (turmeric, chili powder, cumin, coriander)
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional, for garnish)
  • Salt to taste

Instruction

1.

Marinate the meat: In a large bowl, mix the meat with yogurt, minced garlic, grated ginger, chopped green chilies, ground spices (turmeric, chili powder, cumin, coriander), salt, and a tablespoon of oil. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour for the flavors to infuse.

2.

Prepare the rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Then, soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain the rice thoroughly.

3.

Fry the onions: Heat oil or ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add thinly sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish.

4.

Cook the meat: In the same pot with the remaining fried onions, add the marinated meat along with chopped tomatoes. Cook until the meat is partially cooked and the tomatoes are softened.

5.

Layering: Spread the partially cooked meat evenly at the bottom of the pot. Top it with a layer of drained rice. Sprinkle chopped cilantro and mint leaves over the rice. Repeat the layers until all the rice and meat are used up. Ensure that the top layer is rice.

6.

Dum cooking: Dissolve saffron strands in warm milk (optional) and drizzle it over the top layer of rice. Cover the pot tightly with a lid or aluminum foil to seal in the steam. Cook over low heat for about 20-30 minutes, until the rice is fully cooked and tender.

7.

Garnish and serve: Once cooked, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Garnish with the reserved fried onions and additional chopped cilantro and mint leaves. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or salad on the side.
